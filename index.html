Norsk Ordstilling

Norwegian Word Order

Where to place ikke, ofte, alltid, aldri, gjerne, også…

■ Subject (S) ■ Verb (V) ■ Adverb (A) — ikke, ofte… ■ Object (O) ■ Connector / Front element (C)
Main Clause — Normal

Subject first → V2 rule

The verb is always element 2. Adverbs like ikke, ofte, alltid, aldri come after the verb.

S → V → A → O
NorwegianEnglish
Influenseren poster ikke alltid ekte bilder.The influencer doesn't always post real pictures.
Hackerne bruker ofte falske e-poster.The hackers often use fake emails.
Politikeren svarer aldri på spørsmål.The politician never answers questions.
Appen samler alltid data.The app always collects data.
Main Clause — Inversion

Something else first → Subject & verb flip

When anything other than the subject starts the sentence (så, da, derfor, i dag, ofte…), the subject and verb invert. The adverb still comes after the verb.

X → V → S → A → O
NorwegianEnglish
Derfor deler vi ikke alltid passord.That's why we don't always share passwords.
Så poster hun ofte reklame.So she often posts ads.
I dag jobber teamet ikke hjemme.Today the team isn't working from home.
Likevel vinner partiet nesten alltid.Still, the party almost always wins.

⚠️ Test: Is element 1 the subject? No inversion. Is it anything else? Invert S and V.

🔑 The One Rule That Changes Everything

Main clause

Adverb comes after the verb
S → V → ikke → O
Vi bruker ikke VPN.

Subordinate clause

Adverb comes before the verb
S → ikke → V → O
…at vi ikke bruker VPN.
Combining Adverbs

ikke + alltid / ofte / aldri / gjerne

When combining adverbs, the order is almost always ikke + other adverb. Think: "not always" sounds right, "always not" does not.

ikke alltid · ikke ofte · ikke gjerne · ikke lenger
NorwegianEnglish
Vi stoler ikke alltid på algoritmene.We don't always trust the algorithms.
…fordi politikerne ikke alltid sier sannheten.…because politicians don't always tell the truth.
Systemet oppdateres ikke lenger automatisk.The system no longer updates automatically.
Hun deler ikke gjerne private data.She doesn't like sharing private data.

⚠️ Exception: aldri (never) already contains negation — don't combine it with ikke.

Subordinate — hvis (if)

Hvis — "if"

No inversion inside the clause. Adverb comes before the verb.

hvis S A V …, [main clause with inversion]
NorwegianEnglish
Hvis kontoen ikke er sikret, kan hackere komme inn.If the account isn't secured, hackers can get in.
Hvis du ikke alltid bruker tofaktor…If you don't always use two-factor…
Kampanjen lykkes, hvis vi ofte publiserer innhold.The campaign succeeds if we often publish content.

⚠️ When hvis-clause starts the sentence, the main clause gets inversion: Hvis kontoen ikke er sikret, kan hackere komme inn.

Subordinate — da (when/past)

Da — "when" (past event)

As a conjunction, da = "when" (referring to a specific past moment). Adverb before the verb inside.

da S A V …
NorwegianEnglish
Da serveren ikke svarte, sendte vi alarm.When the server didn't respond, we sent an alert.
Da vi endelig oppdaget angrepet…When we finally discovered the attack…
Da politikeren ikke lenger hadde støtte…When the politician no longer had support…

⚠️ Da as an adverb starting a main clause = "then" → triggers inversion: Da kom angrepet.

Subordinate — selv om (even though)

Selv om — "even though / even if"

selv om S A V …
NorwegianEnglish
Selv om vi ikke alltid liker det, må vi oppdatere.Even though we don't always like it, we must update.
Selv om partiet ikke vinner…Even though the party doesn't win…
Vi poster, selv om følgerne ikke alltid reagerer.We post even though followers don't always react.
Subordinate — fordi, når, at, mens, siden

All other conjunctions — same rule

Every subordinate conjunction follows the same pattern: adverb before the verb.

ConjunctionExampleEnglish
fordi (because)fordi de ikke krypterer databecause they don't encrypt data
når (when/whenever)når du alltid bruker VPNwhen you always use VPN
at (that)Jeg tror at han ikke vet detI think he doesn't know it
mens (while)mens systemet ikke er oppewhile the system isn't up
siden (since)siden vi ofte jobber eksterntsince we often work remotely
